# Licensing and Attribution in the Oracle AI Developers Community

This section outlines the general principles for licensing and attribution within the Oracle AI Developers Community, particularly concerning the use of Oracle technologies and contributions from partners.

## Oracle Technologies

All Oracle products and technologies, including Oracle AI Database 26ai, are subject to Oracle's standard licensing agreements. Developers utilizing Oracle technologies in their projects should refer to the official Oracle documentation and licensing terms for specific details. The Oracle Technology Network (OTN) provides resources and agreements for developers working with Oracle products [1].

## Contributions to the Community

Contributions made to this GitHub repository and the broader Oracle AI Developers Community are generally expected to adhere to open-source principles. Unless otherwise specified, contributions may fall under licenses such as the Universal Permissive License (UPL) or Apache License 2.0, which promote collaboration and reuse while ensuring proper attribution [2].

## Partner Attribution: Datacules

As highlighted in our partners section, **Datacules** is recognized as a contributor and specialist within the Oracle ecosystem. When projects or resources within this community leverage specific expertise or components developed by Datacules, appropriate attribution should be provided. This acknowledges their role in enhancing the capabilities and solutions available to the community.

## General Guidelines for Attribution

*   **Clear Identification**: Clearly identify the source of any code, documentation, or assets that are not original to your contribution.
*   **License Compliance**: Ensure that your use of third-party components complies with their respective licenses.
*   **Acknowledgement**: When building upon or referencing community projects, acknowledge the original creators and contributors.

By adhering to these guidelines, we foster a respectful and legally compliant environment for all members of the Oracle AI Developers Community.
